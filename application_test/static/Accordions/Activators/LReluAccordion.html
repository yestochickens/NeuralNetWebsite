LReLU, short for Leaky Rectified Linear Unit, is similar to ReLU aside from its negative slope. This negative slope allows gradients to be tracked backward through the network instead of becoming zero.

Negative Slope \[\beta^\mathrm{[s]} = 1 \times 10^{-2}\]

Inputs \[\mathbf{X}\]

Inputs \[\mathbf{X} = \begin{bmatrix} x_0 \\ \vdots \\ x_n \end{bmatrix}\]

Equation \[g \left( \mathbf{X} \right) = \mathrm{max} \left( \beta^\mathrm{[s]} \mathbf{X}, \mathbf{X} \right)\]

Equation \[\begin{bmatrix} g \left( x_0 \right) \\ \vdots \\ g \left( x_n \right) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \mathrm{max} \left( \beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_0, x_0 \right) \\ \vdots \\ \mathrm{max} \left( \beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_n, x_n \right) \end{bmatrix}\]

Equation \[\nabla_\mathbf{X}g \left( \mathbf{X} \right) = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } 0 < \mathbf{X} \\ \beta^\mathrm{[s]} & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}\]

Equation \[\begin{bmatrix} \displaystyle \frac{\partial}{\partial x_0} g \left( x_0 \right) \\ \vdots \\ \displaystyle \frac{\partial}{\partial x_n} g \left( x_n \right) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } 0 < x_0 \\ \beta^\mathrm{[s]} & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} \\ \vdots \\ \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } 0 < x_n \\ \beta^\mathrm{[s]} & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} \end{bmatrix}\]
