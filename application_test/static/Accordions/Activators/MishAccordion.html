Mish is a more recent activation function that aims to combine the benifits of activations like ReLU and Tanh.

Smoothing \[\beta^\mathrm{[s]} = 1\]

Inputs \[\mathbf{X}\]

Inputs \[\mathbf{X} = \begin{bmatrix} x_0 \\ \vdots \\ x_n \end{bmatrix}\]

Equation \[g \left( \mathbf{X} \right) = \mathbf{X} \circ \mathrm{tanh} \left( \frac{1}{\beta^\mathrm{[s]}} \mathrm{ln} \left (e^{\circ \beta^\mathrm{[s]} \mathbf{X}} + 1 \right) \right)\]

Equation \[\begin{bmatrix} g \left( x_0 \right) \\ \vdots \\ g \left( x_n \right) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \displaystyle x_0 \mathrm{tanh} \left( \frac{\mathrm{ln} \left( e^{\beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_0} + 1\right)}{\beta^\mathrm{[s]}} \right) \\ \vdots \\ \displaystyle x_n \mathrm{tanh} \left( \frac{\mathrm{ln} \left( e^{\beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_n} + 1\right)}{\beta^\mathrm{[s]}} \right) \end{bmatrix}\]

Equation \[\nabla_\mathbf{X}g \left( \mathbf{X} \right) = \mathrm{tanh} \left( \frac{1}{\beta^\mathrm{[s]}} \mathrm{ln} \left( e^{\circ \beta^\mathrm{[s]} \mathbf{X}} + 1\right) \right) + \mathbf{X} \circ e^{\circ \beta^\mathrm{[s]} \mathbf{X}} \circ \mathrm{sech}^2 \left( \frac{1}{\beta^\mathrm{[s]}} \mathrm{ln} \left( e^{\circ \beta^\mathrm{[s]} \mathbf{X}} + 1\right) \right) \oslash \left( e^{\circ \beta^\mathrm{[s]} \mathbf{X}} + 1 \right)\]

Equation \[\begin{bmatrix} \displaystyle \frac{\partial}{\partial x_0} g \left( x_0 \right) \\ \vdots \\ \displaystyle \frac{\partial}{\partial x_n} g \left( x_n \right) \end{bmatrix} = \mathrm{tanh} \left( \frac{1}{\beta^\mathrm{[s]}} \mathrm{ln} \left( \begin{bmatrix} e^{\beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_0} \\ \vdots \\ e^{\beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_n} \end{bmatrix} + 1 \right) \right) + \begin{bmatrix} x_0 \\ \vdots \\ x_n \end{bmatrix} \circ \begin{bmatrix} e^{\beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_0} \\ \vdots \\ e^{\beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_n} \end{bmatrix} \circ \mathrm{sech}^2 \left( \frac{1}{\beta^\mathrm{[s]}} \mathrm{ln} \left( \begin{bmatrix} e^{\beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_0} \\ \vdots \\ e^{\beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_n} \end{bmatrix} + 1\right) \right) \oslash \left( \begin{bmatrix} e^{\beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_0} \\ \vdots \\ e^{\beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_n} \end{bmatrix} + 1 \right)\]
