ReLU, short for Rectified Linear Unit, is one of the most commonly used activation functions due to its simplicity and effectiveness. It provides some of the best results in models and remains computationally efficient.

Inputs \[\mathbf{X}\]

Inputs \[\mathbf{X} = \begin{bmatrix} x_0 \\ \vdots \\ x_n \end{bmatrix}\]

Equation \[g \left( \mathbf{X} \right) = \mathrm{max} \left( 0, \mathbf{X} \right)\]

Equation \[\begin{bmatrix} g \left( x_0 \right) \\ \vdots \\ g \left( x_n \right) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \mathrm{max} \left( 0, x_0 \right) \\ \vdots \\ \mathrm{max} \left( 0, x_n \right) \end{bmatrix}\]

Equation \[\nabla_\mathbf{X}g \left( \mathbf{X} \right) = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } 0 < \mathbf{X} \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}\]

Equation \[\begin{bmatrix} \displaystyle \frac{\partial}{\partial x_0} g \left( x_0 \right) \\ \vdots \\ \displaystyle \frac{\partial}{\partial x_n} g \left( x_n \right) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } 0 < x_0 \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} \\ \vdots \\ \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } 0 < x_n \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} \end{bmatrix}\]
