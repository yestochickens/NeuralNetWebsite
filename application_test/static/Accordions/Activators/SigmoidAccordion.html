Sigmoid is a function that turns an input into a value ranging from zero to one. It's commonly used as a default activation function for people learning about neural networks as it's easier to visualize a neuron ranging from on, one, to off, zero.

Inputs \[\mathbf{X}\]

Inputs \[\mathbf{X} = \begin{bmatrix} x_0 \\ \vdots \\ x_n \end{bmatrix}\]

Equation \[g \left( \mathbf{X} \right) = 1 \oslash \left( e^{\circ -\mathbf{X}} + 1 \right)\]

Equation \[\begin{bmatrix} g \left( x_0 \right) \\ \vdots \\ g \left( x_n \right) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \displaystyle \frac{1}{e^{x_0} + 1} \\ \vdots \\ \displaystyle \frac{1}{e^{x_n} + 1} \end{bmatrix}\]

Equation \[\nabla_\mathbf{X}g \left( \mathbf{X} \right) = e^{\circ -\mathbf{X}} \oslash \left( e^{\circ -\mathbf{X}} + 1\right)^{\circ 2}\]

Equation \[\begin{bmatrix} \displaystyle \frac{\partial}{\partial x_0} g \left( x_0 \right) \\ \vdots \\ \displaystyle \frac{\partial}{\partial x_n} g \left( x_n \right) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} e^{-x_0} \\ \vdots \\ e^{-x_n} \end{bmatrix} \oslash \begin{bmatrix} \left( e^{-x_0} + 1 \right)^2 \\ \vdots \\ \left( e^{-x_n} + 1 \right)^2 \end{bmatrix}\]

Equation \[\begin{bmatrix} \displaystyle \frac{\partial}{\partial x_0} g \left( x_0 \right) \\ \vdots \\ \displaystyle \frac{\partial}{\partial x_n} g \left( x_n \right) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \displaystyle \frac{e^{-x_0}}{\left( e^{-x_0} + 1 \right)^2} \\ \vdots \\ \displaystyle \frac{e^{-x_n}}{\left( e^{-x_n} + 1 \right)^2} \end{bmatrix}\]
