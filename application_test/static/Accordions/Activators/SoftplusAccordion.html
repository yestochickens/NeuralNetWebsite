SoftPlus is the smooth, differentiable-everywhere version of ReLU. Gradients are never resulted to zero in this smooth version of ReLU.

Smoothing \[\beta^\mathrm{[s]} = 1\]

Inputs \[\mathbf{X}\]

Inputs \[\mathbf{X} = \begin{bmatrix} x_0 \\ \vdots \\ x_n \end{bmatrix}\]

Equation \[g \left( \mathbf{X} \right) = \frac{1}{\beta^\mathrm{[s]}} \mathrm{ln} \left( e^{\circ \beta^\mathrm{[s]} \mathbf{X}} + 1 \right)\]

Equation \[\begin{bmatrix} g \left( x_0 \right) \\ \vdots \\ g \left( x_n \right) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \displaystyle \frac{\mathrm{ln} \left( e^{\beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_0} + 1\right)}{\beta^\mathrm{[s]}} \\ \vdots \\ \displaystyle \frac{\mathrm{ln} \left( e^{\beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_n} + 1\right)}{\beta^\mathrm{[s]}} \end{bmatrix}\]

Equation \[\nabla_\mathbf{X}g \left( \mathbf{X} \right) = e^{\circ \beta^\mathrm{[s]} \mathbf{X}} \oslash \left( e^{\circ \beta^\mathrm{[s]} \mathbf{X}} + 1 \right)\]

Equation \[\begin{bmatrix} \displaystyle \frac{\partial}{\partial x_0} g \left( x_0 \right) \\ \vdots \\ \displaystyle \frac{\partial}{\partial x_n} g \left( x_n \right) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} e^{\beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_0} \\ \vdots \\ e^{\beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_n} \end{bmatrix} \oslash \left( \begin{bmatrix} e^{\beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_0} \\ \vdots \\ e^{\beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_n} \end{bmatrix} + 1 \right)\]

Equation \[\begin{bmatrix} \displaystyle \frac{\partial}{\partial x_0} g \left( x_0 \right) \\ \vdots \\ \displaystyle \frac{\partial}{\partial x_n} g \left( x_n \right) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \displaystyle \frac{e^{\beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_0}}{e^{\beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_0} + 1} \\ \vdots \\ \displaystyle \frac{e^{\beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_n}}{e^{\beta^\mathrm{[s]} x_n} + 1} \end{bmatrix}\]
