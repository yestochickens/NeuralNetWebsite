Tanh, short for hyperbolic tangent, is a zero-centered version of the **sigmoid** activation function. It allows for gradients to flow into the negatives.

Inputs \[\mathbf{X}\]

Inputs \[\mathbf{X} = \begin{bmatrix} x_0 \\ \vdots \\ x_n \end{bmatrix}\]

Equation \[g \left( \mathbf{X} \right) = \mathrm{tanh} \left( \mathbf{X} \right)\]

Equation \[\begin{bmatrix} g \left( x_0 \right) \\ \vdots \\ g \left( x_n \right) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \mathrm{tanh} \left( x_0 \right) \\ \vdots \\ \mathrm{tanh} \left( x_n \right) \end{bmatrix}\]

Equation \[\nabla_\mathbf{X}g \left( \mathbf{X} \right) = \mathrm{sech}^2 \left( \mathbf{X} \right)\]

Equation \[\begin{bmatrix} \displaystyle \frac{\partial}{\partial x_0} g \left( x_0 \right) \\ \vdots \\ \displaystyle \frac{\partial}{\partial x_n} g \left( x_n \right) \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \mathrm{sech}^2 \left( x_0 \right) \\ \vdots \\ \mathrm{sech}^2 \left( x_n \right) \end{bmatrix}\]
