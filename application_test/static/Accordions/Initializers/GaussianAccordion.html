Gaussian initialization initializes parameters under a Gaussian curve. It's usually the basis for initialization for parameters that benifit from random initialization, such as initialization for weights.

Mean \[\mu^\mathrm{[s]} = 1\] Standard Deviation \[\sigma^\mathrm{[s]} = 1\] Gain \[\kappa^\mathrm{[s]} = 1\]

Gaussian Distribution \[\mathcal{N} \left( x | \mu^\mathrm{[s]}, \sigma^\mathrm{[s]}\right) = \frac{e^{-\frac{\left( x - \mu^\mathrm{[s]} \right)^2}{2 {\sigma^\mathrm{[s]}}^2}}}{\sqrt{2 \pi {\sigma^\mathrm{[s]}}^2}}\]

Rows \[n\] Columns \[m\]

Initialization \[\mathbf{\Theta_P^\mathrm{[l]}} \sim \kappa^\mathrm{[s]} \mathcal{N} \left( \mu^\mathrm{[s]}, \sigma^\mathrm{[s]}\right), \forall i \in \left[ 1, n \right], j \in \left[ 1, m \right]\]

Initialization \[\begin{bmatrix} \theta_{\mathrm{p}_{0, 0}}^\mathrm{[l]} & \cdots & \theta_{\mathrm{p}_{0, n}}^\mathrm{[l]} \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \theta_{\mathrm{p}_{m, 0}}^\mathrm{[l]} & \cdots & \theta_{\mathrm{p}_{m, n}}^\mathrm{[l]} \\ \end{bmatrix} \sim \begin{bmatrix} \kappa^\mathrm{[s]} \mathcal{N} \left( \mu^\mathrm{[s]}, \sigma^\mathrm{[s]}\right) & \cdots & \kappa^\mathrm{[s]} \mathcal{N} \left( \mu^\mathrm{[s]}, \sigma^\mathrm{[s]}\right) \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \kappa^\mathrm{[s]} \mathcal{N} \left( \mu^\mathrm{[s]}, \sigma^\mathrm{[s]}\right) & \cdots & \kappa^\mathrm{[s]} \mathcal{N} \left( \mu^\mathrm{[s]}, \sigma^\mathrm{[s]}\right) \end{bmatrix}\]
