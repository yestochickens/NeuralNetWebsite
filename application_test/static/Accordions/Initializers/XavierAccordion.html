Xavier initialization is an initialization technique built on Gaussian. It allows for better gradient flow and convergence while training a model.

Mean \[\mu^\mathrm{[s]} = 1\] Standard Deviation \[\sigma^\mathrm{[s]} = 1\] Gain \[\kappa^\mathrm{[s]} = 1\]

Gaussian Distribution \[\mathcal{N} \left( x | \mu^\mathrm{[s]}, \sigma^\mathrm{[s]}\right) = \frac{e^{-\frac{\left( x - \mu^\mathrm{[s]} \right)^2}{2 {\sigma^\mathrm{[s]}}^2}}}{\sqrt{2 \pi {\sigma^\mathrm{[s]}}^2}}\]

Rows \[n\] Columns \[m\]

Initialization \[\mathbf{\Theta_P^\mathrm{[l]}} \sim \kappa^\mathrm{[s]} \sqrt{\frac{2}{n + m}} \mathcal{N} \left( \mu^\mathrm{[s]}, \sigma^\mathrm{[s]}\right), \forall i \in \left[ 1, n \right], j \in \left[ 1, m \right]\]

Initialization \[\begin{bmatrix} \theta_{\mathrm{p}_{0, 0}}^\mathrm{[l]} & \cdots & \theta_{\mathrm{p}_{0, n}}^\mathrm{[l]} \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \theta_{\mathrm{p}_{m, 0}}^\mathrm{[l]} & \cdots & \theta_{\mathrm{p}_{m, n}}^\mathrm{[l]} \\ \end{bmatrix} \sim \begin{bmatrix} \displaystyle \kappa^\mathrm{[s]} \sqrt{\frac{2}{n + m}} \mathcal{N} \left( \mu^\mathrm{[s]}, \sigma^\mathrm{[s]}\right) & \cdots & \displaystyle \kappa^\mathrm{[s]} \sqrt{\frac{2}{n + m}} \mathcal{N} \left( \mu^\mathrm{[s]}, \sigma^\mathrm{[s]}\right) \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \displaystyle \kappa^\mathrm{[s]} \sqrt{\frac{2}{n + m}} \mathcal{N} \left( \mu^\mathrm{[s]}, \sigma^\mathrm{[s]}\right) & \cdots & \displaystyle \kappa^\mathrm{[s]} \sqrt{\frac{2}{n + m}} \mathcal{N} \left( \mu^\mathrm{[s]}, \sigma^\mathrm{[s]}\right) \end{bmatrix}\]
