This documentation attempts to provide thorough definitions and proof of the math behind machine learning. For every part of this documentation (when applicable), the math will first be documented with variable notation and standard operators (ex. \(\mathbf{A} \mathbf{B}\)), then with expanded matrices and vectors and standard operators (ex.

\[ \begin{bmatrix} a_{0, 0} & \cdots & a_{0, n} \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ a_{m, 0} & \cdots & a_{m, n} \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} b_{0, 0} & \cdots & b_{0, o} \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ b_{n, 0} & \cdots & b_{n, o} \end{bmatrix} \]

), then with expanded matrices and vectors and linear operators (ex.

\[ \begin{bmatrix} \sum_{i=0}^n \left( a_{0, i} \cdot b_{i, 0} \right) & \cdots & \sum_{i=0}^n \left( a_{0, i} \cdot b_{i, o} \right) \\ \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ \sum_{i=0}^n \left( a_{m, i} \cdot b_{i, 0} \right) & \cdots & \sum_{i=0}^n \left( a_{m, i} \cdot b_{i, o} \right) \end{bmatrix} \]

After the definitions, proofs of each derived definition will be provided if applicable.
