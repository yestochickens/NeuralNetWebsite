SSR, short for Sum of the Squared Residuals, is a commonly used loss function due to its easily interpreted loss calculation, squaring the difference between the true and predicted labels. It can be used outside of binary classification, and its squaring gives more weight to larger errors and allows errors to add up, rather than cancel each other out.

Predicted \[\begin{bmatrix} \widehat{y_0} \\ \vdots \\ \widehat{y_{n_y}} \end{bmatrix}\] Expected \[\begin{bmatrix} y_0 \\ \vdots \\ y_{n_y} \end{bmatrix}\]

Equation \[J \left( \mathbf{\hat{Y}}, \mathbf{Y} \right) = \sum_{i=1}^{n_y} \left( y_i - \widehat{y_i} \right)^2\]
