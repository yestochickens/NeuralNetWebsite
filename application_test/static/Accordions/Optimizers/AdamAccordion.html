Adam, short for Adaptive Moment Estimation, is an optimization algorithm that combines features of the Root Mean Squared Propagation optimization algorithm and the Adaptive Gradient Algorithm optimization algorithm. Its one of the most widely used optimization algorithms due to its convergence speed and computational efficieny. Adam's first moment results in smooth descent with optimization and helps locate global, rather than local minima. Adam's second moment estimate variance and allow for adaptive learning rates. The zero-bias correction allows for initial few steps to be appropriately scaled. The AMSGrad variant of Adam attempts prevent convergence issues with Adam by taking the maximum observed second moment rather than the current estimated second moment.
